Seasonality¶

Based on the aggregated transaction volume by month, there doesn't seem to be a strong seasonal effect at the month, quarter level, which is period of interest for this exercise. Seasonal effects may exist at the weekly or merchant level, and if they do, they will be captured with merchant level features for modeling

Takeaways¶

The total transaction volume, number of merchants, number of transactions on the platform are steadily growing. While the average transaction amount is steady following an early bout of volatility, the median transaction frequency moves around a fair bit over time. Certain algorithms (e.g., decision trees) don't generalize well on long term trends, though the extent to which this is an issue will depend on if the trends are observed within merchants as well, and can be addressed via feature engineering (assigning relative ranks and using comparisons to their own past characteristics). We'll explore this some more when we're doing feature engineering.

There don't seem to be strong seasonal effects at the aggregate level, though these may be evident for some merchants.

As expected, across merchants the total transaction volume, number of transactions, volatility, and to a lesser extent the transaction frequency follow a power law distribution.

Unexpectedly, a significant proportion of merchants go many months between transactions - we'll have to be deliberate with our churn assignments.

Lastly, while there are outliers, they appear to be organic accounts.
